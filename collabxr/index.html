Contributions

The outline of the contributions of this literature review is threefold:

  • a systematic analysis of paper types, research topics, evaluation scenarios, cognitive aspects that emerge from data collection methods, and configurations of evaluations in XR collaboration.
  • an overview of some of the key challenges presented in the recent literature and provide a summary of related research work.
  • a publicly available website that includes interactive visualization, source codes, and all the data set of the data collected in our system-atic literature analysis

Method

  • We excluded publications that included the search terms but were not aimed at understanding, testing, or implementing XR collaboration - the search terms were associated with different aims.
  • We excluded publications that included duplicates between the two libraries, or publications where the same or similar results were presented in different venues or publication formats. For example, the same work might have appeared in a poster or extended abstract form, and later published in a more extensive proceeding or journal article. In such cases, we included only the final relative comprehensive publication, omitting the earlier preliminary publications.
  • We excluded publications that focused on collaboration but not associate with XR. While some publications on collaboration explored the impact of their integration into the industry, we excluded them from this review.
